The Fastest Peanut Butter Cookie Recipe
<-Return

Having posted no other recipes before, this peanut butter cookie recipe is among the top three most popular recipes I have shared. I was given this recipe many years ago by my good friend Google who directed me to Allrecipes. Specifically to this recipe
https://www.allrecipes.com/recipe/244614/3-ingredient-peanut-butter-cookies/
Since then, I have made and eaten these cookies numerous times. They only require three ingredients that you likely have at home!

I remember making these cookies a long, long time ago, which many of you will not remember, 2020. For those of you who were too young to remember this time, it was an age of isolation. During this time, many things came to a halt causing people to have an absurd amount of free time. I chose to use my free time to make desserts, such as peanut butter cookies. I often made them with my younger sister, which is how I can guarantee that this recipe is foolproof.

This cookie recipe is one of my favorites for fast and easy cookies. They take less than 30 minutes total, so they are great for when you suddenly need something sweet or accidntally procrastinated on making something for an event. It only takes 3 ingredients, 3 steps, and 30 minutes. Anyone can make them!

Step 1:

Gather your ingredients. Brave your fridge and pantry to find one egg, one cup of sugar, and one cup of peanut butter. Once you have finished scavenging for your ingredients, combine them in a large bowl. The ingredients should look combined when finished. If you can see the individual ingredients, you have not mixed it well. Do better, please.

Image: Michell Becker

Notes:

I commonly see people asking what substitution can be made in this recipe. Since there are so few ingredients in this recipe, I recommend only substituting comfort for grocery shopping. If you happen to be allergic to peanuts, you cannot exclude the peanut butter from the recipe. I repeat, do not make cookies with only eggs and sugar. However, nut-free peanut butter is an acceptable substitution.

Step 2:

Image: Preethi Venkatram

Now that everything has been combined to form dough, preheat the oven to 350°F. While your oven is preheating, prepare a cookie tray with parchment paper. Roll the dough into balls and place on the baking tray leaving an inch between them. Flatten the dough slightly, being sure to not make too flat. Making your dough resemble pancakes before baking could cause burning. Good enough for fancy looking cookies? Use a fork to lightly press a grid into the dough after flattening.

Step 3:

Put baking the tray into the preheated oven. Do not touch the oven with your bare hands as it will be hot. (Approximately 350°F of hot. Human hands are weak.) Close the oven door and let the cookies bake for 10 minutes. Take this time to clean up the crime mess you have inevitably just created. If you do not, the cookies will judge you.

Enjoy!

Remove the cookies from the oven using an oven mitt and let them cool before eating. I would recommend waiting at least five minutes to let them cool, however this is your life, and I am in no position to tell you how to live it. Eat burning hot cookies if it will bring you joy.

Recipe Notes:

To encourage the cookies to cool faster out of the oven, you could give them words of encouragement or simply remove them from the baking tray to a cooling rack or plate. Once cooled you can store these cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 4 days (give or take 4 days) or freeze them to make them last longer.
